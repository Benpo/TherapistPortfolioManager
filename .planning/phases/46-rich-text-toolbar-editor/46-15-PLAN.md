<objective>
Round-2 gap fix for Phase 46. The 46-14 real-device re-run (2026-07-15, MacBook Pro, build 00f0c08) failed checklist item 11 and produced two new high-severity gaps (46-UAT.md gaps 10 and 11). This plan fixes BOTH with a single scoped CSS change to the export Step-2 editing surface, proven by a new falsifiable WebKit layout probe.

**Gap 10 — Step-2 default layout collapses on laptop viewports.** `.export-card.is-editor-step` is hard-sized to `block-size: 50dvh` (app.css ~3360). On a ~820-980px laptop viewport that is a ~410-490px card; the fixed Step-2 chrome inside it (title, step indicator, "Step 2 of 3" helper, ephemeral-edit info note, actions row) consumes ~330-380px, so `.export-edit-area` (flex:1, min-block-size:0, overflow-y:auto — app.css ~3384) collapses to ~50-100px. Inside that collapsed column the toolbar (a default-shrinkable flex child with `overflow-y:hidden`, app.css ~5360) compresses and clips its buttons to slivers; the editor's 240px min-height overflows the tiny scroll area showing ~1.5 lines. The fix makes the EDIT SURFACE the sizing floor, not the card.

**Gap 11 — export toolbar not ALWAYS visible.** Ben re-affirmed verbatim: "the formatting toolbar should never be hidden in the export." Two mechanisms still hide the persistent bar: (a) compression clipping per Gap 10 (no flex-shrink:0); (b) scroll-away — rich-toolbar.js docks the persistent bar `beforebegin` the textarea, i.e. INSIDE `.export-edit-area`, which is the `overflow-y:auto` scroll container, so scrolling a document taller than the area moves the bar out of view. The fix pins the bar (sticky) and stops it compressing (flex-shrink:0).

Purpose: make the export Step-2 default surface usable and keep its toolbar always visible, without disturbing the ratified 50%/90% sizing, the mobile takeover, or the 7 note-field toolbars.
Output: a scoped app.css change plus a RED-then-GREEN Playwright-WebKit layout probe.
