Step 1

Adding a client

Every session belongs to a client. From the overview you open the new-client form and enter as little or as much as you like — a name is enough to begin. Anything else can be filled in later, in your own time.

Your client list stays on this device and is sorted so the people you are working with right now are easy to find. Search and filters help when the list grows.

Make it yours

Making Sessions Garden yours

No two practitioners work the same way, so the session page bends to fit you rather than the other way around. Each section — context notes, emotions, the Heart Shield, severity, your own free-text boxes — can be switched on or off.

Turn off what you never use and the page becomes shorter and quieter. Turn a section back on whenever your practice changes. Your layout is remembered for every future session, so the app keeps feeling like the way you already work.

Why this is here so early: shaping the page to your own method makes everything that follows feel like yours, not a form to fill in.

Step 2

Starting a session

Opening a session for a client brings up a fresh page dated for today, with a short summary of where you left off last time so you can pick up the thread.

You can write as you go or after the session ends — everything is saved on this device as you type, with nothing to submit and nothing to lose.

Step 3

Capturing emotions

When you identify a trapped emotion, you can paste or type it straight into the session — including a whole list at once. Each one becomes its own entry you can revisit and track.

This keeps the rhythm of the session intact: you stay with your client and the notes keep up with you, rather than the other way around.

Step 4

The Heart Shield workflow

The Heart Shield has its own dedicated space in the session, kept distinct from ordinary trapped emotions so the work around the heart stays clearly marked.

You record each layer as you uncover it, and the running picture stays visible across sessions so progress with the heart is never lost between visits.

Step 5

Tracking severity, before and after

For an issue you are following, you can note its intensity before the work and again afterwards, so the shift within a single session is plain to see.

If a number moves the wrong way, that reversal is kept as part of the record rather than smoothed over — the honest picture is the useful one, and it is there next time you open this client.

Step 6

Finishing, reviewing and exporting

At the end you read the session back, make any final notes, and close it. A single session can be exported on its own — one to send to your client, and one to file in your own records.

Each export is a self-contained document. Nothing is uploaded; the file is created here and goes only where you choose to send it.

Step 7

Back to the overview

Closing a session returns you to the overview, where recent clients and their progress are gathered in one calm place ready for the next time.

From here the loop begins again — and you can reopen this guide, or the guided tour, whenever you would like a refresher.
